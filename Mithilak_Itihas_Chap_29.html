Q1. Chapter VIII is titled:
Explanation: The contents name Chapter VIII as The Age of the Muslim Conquest.
Q2. The period given for Chapter VIII is:
Explanation: The contents give 1200–1556 A.D.
Q3. What is the chapter’s broad theme?
Explanation: The title and preface describe a chronological review of the Muslim conquest of Mithilā.
Q4. Which earlier dynasty’s end is connected to this age?
Explanation: The Karnata fall and Harisiṃhadeva’s displacement form an important background.
Q5. Which later medieval line is also relevant to the transition before 1526?
Explanation: The Oinavāras ruled in the later medieval period before the Mughal age.
Q6. What terminal year does the chapter use?
Explanation: The contents give 1556 A.D. as the end point.
Q7. What wider north Indian political process frames this chapter?
Explanation: The conquest age belongs to the period of Sultanate expansion in north India.
Q8. Why is Chapter VIII placed after “Life and Condition of the people”?
Explanation: The preface says Chapter VIII gives a chronological review of the Muslim conquest after social-cultural discussion.
Q9. What kind of evidence is especially useful for this chapter?
Explanation: The book’s method uses chronicles and local records for medieval political events.
Q10. Which place becomes important after Harisiṃhadeva’s displacement?
Explanation: The Karnata chapter and source discussion connect Harisiṃhadeva with Nepal after the invasion.
Q11. Which statement best fits the author’s approach to conquest?
Explanation: The preface says the author presents a chronological review based on materials.
Q12. What is the significance of 1200 A.D. in the chapter title?
Explanation: The contents place the conquest age from 1200 A.D.
Q13. What is the significance of 1556 A.D. as an endpoint?
Explanation: 1556 A.D. is a conventional boundary for the beginning of Akbar’s effective rule.
Q14. Which earlier chapter gives immediate dynastic background for Chapter VIII?
Explanation: Chapter VI supplies the late medieval dynasty before the conquest-age close.
Q15. What happened to Mithilā’s independent position by the later medieval period according to the preface?
Explanation: The preface says the first four chapters cover subjugation and later chapters show local monarchy followed by conquest.
Q16. Which event from Chapter V connects directly with Muslim conquest?
Explanation: The Karnata ending leads into the medieval conquest context.
Q17. Why are Muslim historians mentioned in the source chapter?
Explanation: The source chapter notes Muslim historians and travellers give information by reference to medieval Mithilā and invasions.
Q18. Which of the following is not the main subject of Chapter VIII?
Explanation: The derivation of Videha is Chapter I, not Chapter VIII.
Q19. What is Chapter VIII’s place in the whole book?
Explanation: The contents show Chapter VIII as the final chapter before the index.
