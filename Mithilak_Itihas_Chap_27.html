Chapter VI — The Oinavāras

Instant feedback: wrong answers turn red, correct answers turn green. Explanations open immediately.

Index 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
Q1. Chapter VI is titled:
Explanation: The contents name Chapter VI as The Oinavāras.
Q2. The Oinavāra period covers approximately:
Explanation: The contents give circa 1353–1526 A.D.
Q3. What is the first topic under the Oinavāras?
Explanation: The chapter begins with their origin.
Q4. Which ruler is listed first in the Oinavāra sequence?
Explanation: Kāmeśvara Ṭhākura appears first.
Q5. Who follows Kāmeśvara Ṭhākura in the contents?
Explanation: Bhogīśvara Ṭhākura is listed after Kāmeśvara.
Q6. Which ruler comes after Bhogīśvara Ṭhākura?
Explanation: Gaṇeśvara Ṭhākura is in the sequence.
Q7. Which ruler is linked with the poet Vidyāpati in common Mithilā tradition and is listed in this chapter?
Explanation: Kīrti Siṃha is an Oinavāra ruler; Vidyāpati’s Kīrtilatā is associated with this period.
Q8. Which ruler follows Kīrti Siṃha in the contents?
Explanation: Bhavasimha Deva is listed after Kīrti Siṃha.
Q9. Which ruler follows Bhavasimha Deva?
Explanation: Devasimha follows Bhavasimha Deva.
Q10. Which important Oinavāra ruler is listed after Devasimha?
Explanation: Śivasiṃha is an important ruler of this period.
Q11. Which later ruler is listed after Padmasiṃha?
Explanation: The contents list Padmasiṃha followed by Viśvāsa Deva.
Q12. Which ruler is listed after Viśvāsa Deva?
Explanation: The Oinavāra list includes a Harisiṃhadeva distinct in context from the earlier Karnata Harisiṃhadeva.
Q13. Which rulers appear near the end of the sequence?
Explanation: These are named in the final part of the Oinavāra list.
Q14. What does “The Later Oinavāras” indicate?
Explanation: The later section covers the end of Oinavāra rule.
Q15. Which chapter immediately follows the Oinavāras?
Explanation: Chapter VII examines society and culture during the medieval period.
Q16. What form of history is Chapter VI mainly?
Explanation: It traces rulers and succession of the Oinavāra line.
Q17. The Oinavāras are described in the preface as:
Explanation: The preface calls Chapter VI the age of the Oinavāra Brāhmaṇa-kings.
Q18. Which literary-cultural name is especially associated with this era?
Explanation: The chapter’s period is the context for Vidyāpati and medieval Maithili culture.
Q19. What is the chronological place of the Oinavāras?
Explanation: The book places them between the Karnatas and the Muslim conquest.
Q20. Best summary of Chapter VI:
Explanation: The contents list origin and a long succession of Oinavāra rulers.
