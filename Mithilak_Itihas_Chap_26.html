Chapter V — The Karnatas

Instant feedback: wrong answers turn red, correct answers turn green. Explanations open immediately.

Index 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
Q1. Chapter V is devoted to which dynasty?
Explanation: The chapter is titled The Karnatas.
Q2. The Karnata chapter covers approximately:
Explanation: The contents give 1097–1324 A.D.
Q3. Which ruler begins the Karnata line in the contents?
Explanation: Nānyadeva is the first major Karnata ruler listed.
Q4. What topic follows “The Karnatas” in the contents?
Explanation: The chapter includes a section on their origin.
Q5. Which important administrative-cultural practice is linked with the Karnata period in the book?
Explanation: The source chapter notes the Panji begins in Śaka 1235/1313 A.D. in Harisiṃhadeva’s reign.
Q6. Which ruler is listed after Nānyadeva?
Explanation: Gaṅgadeva follows Nānyadeva in the contents.
Q7. Which ruler is listed after Gaṅgadeva?
Explanation: Narasiṃhadeva is in the Karnata list.
Q8. Which ruler follows Narasiṃhadeva?
Explanation: Rāmasiṃhadeva appears after Narasiṃhadeva.
Q9. Which ruler follows Rāmasiṃhadeva?
Explanation: Śaktisiṃhadeva is listed in the chapter.
Q10. Which last Karnata ruler is prominently connected with the end of the line?
Explanation: Harisiṃhadeva is the last ruler listed before the invasion of Nepal.
Q11. Which event is listed at the end of Chapter V?
Explanation: The contents end the chapter with the invasion of Nepal.
Q12. What does “Date and Accession” concern in this chapter?
Explanation: The chapter addresses the date and accession of the Karnata founder.
Q13. Which theme is central in the Karnata chapter?
Explanation: The Karnatas mark a renewed local dynastic phase.
Q14. What is the chronological relationship of Karnatas to Oinavāras?
Explanation: Chapter V comes before Chapter VI, the Oinavāras.
Q15. Which invasion affected the end of the Karnata period?
Explanation: The end of the Karnatas is linked with fourteenth-century Muslim power and Harisiṃhadeva’s move toward Nepal.
Q16. Which title best fits the chapter’s focus?
Explanation: The chapter is a dynastic account.
Q17. Why is Harisiṃhadeva important beyond politics?
Explanation: The book connects Harisiṃhadeva with later Mithilā social customs and genealogical records.
Q18. Which pair belongs to Chapter V, not Chapter VI?
Explanation: Nānyadeva and Harisiṃhadeva are Karnata rulers.
Q19. What follows the Karnata period in the book?
Explanation: The next chapter covers the Oinavāras.
Q20. Best summary of Chapter V:
Explanation: The contents present the full sequence of Karnata rulers and end of the line.
