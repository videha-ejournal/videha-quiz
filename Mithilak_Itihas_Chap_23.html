Chapter II — The Early Monarchy: The Videhas

Q1. The early monarchy chapter covers approximately which period?
Explanation: The contents mark Chapter II as the Videhas, circa 3000 B.C.–circa 600 B.C.
Q2. Who are the central people of Chapter II?
Explanation: The chapter is explicitly titled The Videhas.
Q3. Which royal line is treated in this chapter?
Explanation: A major section is titled The Janaka Dynasty.
Q4. Who is called the great philosopher-king?
Explanation: The contents and text identify Kṛti Janaka as the great philosopher-king.
Q5. Which sage is closely associated with Janaka’s court and philosophical debates?
Explanation: The book links Janaka’s court with Yājñavalkya and debates in the Bṛhadāraṇyaka tradition.
Q6. What role does Śatānanda have in the chapter’s narrative?
Explanation: The contents list The Śatānanda Line among early Videha topics.
Q7. The book says Videha became prominent after civilisation shifted in which direction?
Explanation: The early chapter relates Videha’s rise to the eastward movement of Vedic culture.
Q8. Which capital is repeatedly connected with the Videhas?
Explanation: The city of Mithilā/Janakapura is treated as the political and cultural centre.
Q9. Which source depicts Janaka as a philosopher-king through dialogues?
Explanation: The philosophical Janaka is chiefly drawn from Upaniṣadic traditions.
Q10. What does the book say about the exact historicity of the earliest Janakas?
Explanation: The author repeatedly distinguishes historical probability from legendary lists.
Q11. Which section follows Kṛti Janaka in the contents?
Explanation: Chapter II includes a section on relations with neighbouring powers.
Q12. What topic is treated under “The Later Videhas”?
Explanation: The later Videha section concerns the later stages of the Videha monarchy.
Q13. Which theme is included after popular choice and advisers?
Explanation: The chapter does not stop at kingship; it includes society and economy.
Q14. What does the chapter say about trade and industry?
Explanation: Trade and industry are explicitly listed in the chapter contents.
Q15. Which intellectual field is listed at the end of the chapter?
Explanation: Religion and philosophy are included in the survey of early Videha culture.
Q16. What is the author’s view of Janaka as a name?
Explanation: The text discusses Janaka as a dynastic/family designation in several traditions.
Q17. Which epic figure is connected with the Janaka tradition?
Explanation: The chapter connects Janaka/Siradhvaja with Sītā traditions.
Q18. What type of government is Chapter II mainly about?
Explanation: The chapter title and contents identify it as the early monarchy.
Q19. What does the author do with long royal lists?
Explanation: The treatment of Janaka genealogy compares multiple textual traditions.
Q20. Which broad conclusion fits Chapter II?
Explanation: The chapter presents ancient Videha as politically and intellectually significant.
